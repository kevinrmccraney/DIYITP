The DIY ITP Project

NYU's Interactive Telecommunications Program costs $25,093 per term. Using freely available resources, you can get (almost) the same education for free.

Explanation

This list of courses comes from ITP NYU, an interdisciplinary masters degree in art and technology. I was interested in taking this degree a few years back, but--what with the rising cost of college and the price of living in New York--I couldn't justify it. So, I did the next best thing: I got on the internet. ITP offers a fantastic list of past courses.

For this list, I've done my best to select only courses that are well-documented and lead to practical skills--none of the seminar-based, theoretical, reading intensive courses. You can immerse yourself in those using your local library (or the 'further reading' section below). I also opted to include things that have a very clear syllabus and minimal equipment requirements.

People frequently debate the relevance of MFA programs. In almost every discipline, they argue that the coursework is irrelevant, that the program is about making connections and honing your skills. Remember that you're not whatever piece of paper you earn, you're the effort you put in to get it. Build a portfolio. Show your work.

First Tier: Foundational Courses

Do all of these. You might know how to do some of the work in this section already; it's considered survey material.

  • Intro to Computational Media
  • Intro to Physical Computing
  • Visual Language
  • Networked Media

Second Tier: Workshops and Seminars

Do ten of these. They are considered electives. You can take courses from other departments within the university, so feel free to find some other online courses and try those.

  • Aesthetics of Automation
  • Basic Analog Circuits
  • Automata
  • Computational Cameras
  • Culinary Physics
  • Designing for Digital Fabrication
  • Digital Writing with Python
  • Hacking Higher Ed
  • Introduction to Dataflow Programming
  • Mobile Web
  • The Nature of Code
  • Parametric Design for Digital Fabrication
  • Printing Code
  • Energy
  • Programming from A to Z
  • Sensors: The Basics
  • Visualizing Data

Third Tier: Thesis Project

To paraphrase the degree requirements, you should make a project and document your process. Then, present it to a group of people. Some of them should be familiar with the kind of work you do, and some should be unfamiliar with it. Share this work with your friends and people in the community, like a real artist!
